Here's the prompt:

"Please audit this entire website and eliminate every pattern that makes it look AI-generated or templated. The goal is for this site to look like it was designed by a boutique agency and built by hand. Do the following:

1. Typography — make it feel considered

Replace any generic system font stack with a curated pairing from Google Fonts — use one serif or high-contrast display font for headings and one clean sans-serif for body (e.g. Fraunces + DM Sans, Playfair Display + Inter, or Cormorant + Nunito — pick what fits the brand)
Add optical sizing, letter-spacing, and line-height values that feel intentional — headlines should feel editorial, not default
Remove any instances of sentence case headings that feel like ChatGPT output — vary the heading rhythm
2. Layout — break the grid intentionally

Add at least one section that breaks out of the standard centered column — an asymmetric split, an overlapping element, a full-bleed image with text inset, or a staggered card layout
Remove any section that is just: heading, subheading, three equal columns with icons, paragraph. That pattern is the fingerprint of AI-generated sites
Add negative space deliberately — not every section needs a background color change
Make the hero feel specific to this business, not a template — no stock-looking layouts
3. Color & texture

Audit the color palette — if it's just blue/white/grey with a teal accent, make it more specific. Pull a palette that feels ownable and intentional
Add at least one subtle texture or grain overlay, a soft gradient, or a non-rectangular shape (diagonal section break, blob, or clipped element) to break up flat sections
Ensure hover states on all buttons and links feel custom — not just opacity changes
4. Copywriting — remove AI tells

Find and replace every instance of the following words and phrases: 'comprehensive', 'cutting-edge', 'state-of-the-art', 'seamless', 'tailored', 'leverage', 'innovative', 'solutions', 'dedicated to', 'committed to', 'ensuring', 'we strive to', 'look no further', 'journey', 'transformative', 'passionate about'
Rewrite any bullet list that starts with a gerund (Providing, Offering, Delivering, Ensuring) — these are AI copy fingerprints
Add one piece of specific, concrete detail per service — something that could only be true about this specific practice, not a generic dental office
Make the tone feel like a real person wrote it — conversational where appropriate, specific rather than broad
5. Micro-details that signal craft

Add subtle entrance animations on scroll (fade up, slight translate) using CSS or a lightweight library — but keep them fast (under 400ms) and purposeful, not decorative
Add a custom focus style for accessibility that also looks intentional
Make sure buttons have a distinct pressed/active state
Add a subtle box-shadow or border treatment to cards that feels designed, not defaulted
Check that all section padding is consistent and intentional — no section should feel cramped or accidentally spacious
6. Images

Add object-fit: cover and intentional object-position to all images so they feel art-directed
If any images are generic stock photos of smiling people in dental chairs, flag them for replacement with more specific, editorial-feeling photography
Add a subtle image treatment (slight desaturation, warm tone overlay, or vignette) to make photos feel cohesive rather than mismatched
7. Footer & details

Make the footer feel designed — not just a stack of links and a copyright line. Add a short brand statement, a visual element, or a layout that reflects the overall design language
Ensure the 404 page is custom and on-brand, not the framework default
When done, give me a list of every change made by file, flag anything that needs real photography or copy input from the client, and tell me if any third-party libraries were added so I know what's in the bundle."
